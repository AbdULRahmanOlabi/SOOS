Why Us

Established For Two Decades, Our Syrian Physical School Has Been Successfully Delivering The Syrian Curriculum To Students On Campus. Now, We Are Proud To Share Our Expertise and Innovative Teaching Methods Beyond Our Local Border. Through This Platform Virtual School Syrian Oasis Online School

The Goal of The Organization is To Provide Comprehensive Education To Children Regardless of Their Location, With a Focus on Compensating For Educational Gaps and Promoting The Arabic Language. We Aim To Enhance Scientific Research and Innovation and Promote a Culture of Innovation in The Work Environment While Achieving High Efficiency Among Administrative and Educational Teams

  • Our Goal is To Remain At The Forefront of Intellectual and Technological Progress, Making a Significant Impact in The Realm of Virtual Educational
  • We are Dedicated To Following Contemporary Learning Strategies That Promote Exploration, Analysis and Problem-Solving Skills To Inspire Student To Innovate and Succeed

Our Accredited Curriculum Syrian Curriculum With Modern Teaching Methods, Covering Preparatory To Third Secondary School in Scientific and Literary Branches, and Provides Regular Certificates Approved By The Syrian Ministry of Education. Furthermore, We Also Issue a School Hierarchy For Students, Allowing Them To Transfer To Any School Within and Outside Syria. With The Use of an Electronic Sounding System, We Help Students Without Academic Degrees From Previous Years Catch Up. Our Team of Teacher is Equipped With Modern Teaching Methods and Interactive Techniques To Deliver an Engaging Educational Experience

Our Programs

Curriculum Category (A): This is an approved curriculum by the Syrian Ministry of Education designed to compensate for the age and educational gaps of students in basic education, covering students from the first grade of elementary school to the second grade of middle school, within the age range of 8 to 15 years. It aims to prepare students for legal and effective integration into the general curriculum. Students cover the material for two grades in one year, spread across four levels as follows: Level 1: Includes the first and second grades. Level 2: Includes the third and fourth grades. Level 3: Includes the fifth and sixth grades. Level 4: Includes the seventh and eighth grades. Students are assessed with a basic skills level assessment, after which they are placed at the appropriate level. The assessment is done electronically.
